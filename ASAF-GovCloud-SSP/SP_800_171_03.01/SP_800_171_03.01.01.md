# SP_800_171_03.01.01 - \[Access Control\] Account Management

## Control Statement

- \[SR-03.01.01.a\] Define the types of system accounts allowed and prohibited.

- \[SR-03.01.01.b\] Create, enable, modify, disable, and remove system accounts in accordance with policy, procedures, prerequisites, and criteria.

- \[SR-03.01.01.c\] Specify:

  - \[SR-03.01.01.c.01\] Authorized users of the system,
  - \[SR-03.01.01.c.02\] Group and role membership, and
  - \[SR-03.01.01.c.03\] Access authorizations (i.e., privileges) for each account.

- \[SR-03.01.01.d\] Authorize access to the system based on:

  - \[SR-03.01.01.d.01\] A valid access authorization and
  - \[SR-03.01.01.d.02\] Intended system usage.

- \[SR-03.01.01.e\] Monitor the use of system accounts.

- \[SR-03.01.01.f\] Disable system accounts when:

  - \[SR-03.01.01.f.01\] The accounts have expired,
  - \[SR-03.01.01.f.02\] The accounts have been inactive for [time period],
  - \[SR-03.01.01.f.03\] The accounts are no longer associated with a user or individual,
  - \[SR-03.01.01.f.04\] The accounts are in violation of organizational policy, or
  - \[SR-03.01.01.f.05\] Significant risks associated with individuals are discovered.

- \[SR-03.01.01.g\] Notify account managers and designated personnel or roles within:

  - \[SR-03.01.01.g.01\] [time period] when accounts are no longer required.
  - \[SR-03.01.01.g.02\] [time period] when users are terminated or transferred.
  - \[SR-03.01.01.g.03\] [time period] when system usage or the need-to-know changes for an individual.

- \[SR-03.01.01.h\] Require that users log out of the system after [time period] of expected inactivity or when [circumstances].

## Control guidance

This requirement focuses on account management for systems and applications. The definition and enforcement of access authorizations other than those determined by account type (e.g., privileged access, non-privileged access) are addressed in [](#/cprt/framework/version/SP_800_171_3_0_0/home?element=03.01.02)03.01.02. System account types include individual, group, temporary, system, guest, anonymous, emergency, developer, and service. Users who require administrative privileges on system accounts receive additional scrutiny by personnel responsible for approving such accounts and privileged access. Types of accounts that organizations may prohibit due to increased risk include group, emergency, guest, anonymous, and temporary. Organizations may choose to define access privileges or other attributes by account, type of account, or a combination of both. Other attributes required for authorizing access include restrictions on the time of day, day of the week, and point of origin. When defining other system account attributes, organizations consider system requirements (e.g., system upgrades, scheduled maintenance) and mission and business requirements (e.g., time zone differences, remote access to facilitate travel requirements). Users who pose a significant security risk include individuals for whom reliable evidence indicates either the intention to use authorized access to the system to cause harm or that adversaries will cause harm through them. Close coordination among mission and business owners, system administrators, human resource managers, and legal staff is essential when disabling system accounts for high-risk individuals. Time periods for the notification of organizational personnel or roles may vary. Inactivity logout is behavior- or policy-based and requires users to take physical action to log out when they are expecting inactivity longer than the defined period. Automatic enforcement of inactivity logout is addressed by [](#/cprt/framework/version/SP_800_171_3_0_0/home?element=03.01.10) 03.01.10.

### This System

**GovCloud ECS (ASAF API) — CUI Asset — implemented**

Amazon Cognito User Pool `secred-asaf-users` enforces account lifecycle management for all CUI-boundary users. Account types are restricted to: individual, service. Prohibited types (group, emergency, guest, anonymous, temporary) are blocked by Cognito configuration and the `DenyCUIWithoutUSPersonTag` SCP on the Prod OU.

- **[a] Allowed/prohibited types**: Cognito allows Individual and Service account types only. SCP `DenyCUIWithoutUSPersonTag` prevents resource access without `us_person=true` tag. Prohibited types cannot be created in the User Pool.
- **[b] Create/enable/modify/disable/remove**: Managed through Cognito Admin API, Terraform IaC (`deploy/govcloud/terraform/`), and the `souhimbou-admin` IAM user with MFA. All account changes emit CloudTrail events (`secred-govcloud-org-trail`).
- **[c] Authorized users / roles / privileges**: IAM Identity Center `CUIWorkloadAccess` permission set maps USPersons group to the SecDev225 account. ABAC attribute `us_person=true` enforced on all CUI resource access. RBAC roles (Admin, ComplianceOfficer, SecurityEngineer, Operator, Viewer) defined in `pkg/auth/provider.go:193-299`.
- **[d] Access authorization**: Access requires valid Cognito JWT with `us_person` claim (injected by `secred-cognito-pretok` Lambda pre-token trigger) AND valid `us_person=true` ABAC tag on the requesting identity.
- **[e] Monitor account use**: CloudTrail logs all Cognito and IAM Identity Center events. GuardDuty monitors for anomalous access patterns. Security Hub aggregates findings.
- **[f] Disable on inactivity**: Cognito advanced security mode (ENFORCED) with automated response. Accounts inactive for 90 days are disabled via automated Lambda review (Step 6A). Accounts of terminated personnel are disabled within 24 hours per SECRED-POL-001.
- **[g] Notifications**: Account managers notified via SNS `secred-cloudtrail-alerts` within 24 hours when accounts are no longer required or when users are terminated/transferred; 72 hours when need-to-know changes.
- **[h] Session logout**: Cognito access token validity: 1 hour. Refresh token: 24 hours. ASAF session termination enforced after 15 minutes of inactivity (03.01.11).

**Hostinger VPS — Out-of-Scope**

The commercial VPS tier does not process, store, or transmit CUI. It is logically separated from the GovCloud CUI boundary by design. No account management requirements apply to this component for CMMC purposes.

**Developer Workstations — CRMA**

Windows 11 workstations used by SecRed Knowledge Inc. personnel. Account management enforced by Windows local policy and Bitdefender endpoint protection. Personnel subject to SECRED-POL-001 US Person vetting before GovCloud access is provisioned.
