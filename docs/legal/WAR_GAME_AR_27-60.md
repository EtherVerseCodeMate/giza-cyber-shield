## SCENARIO A: THE "HOSTILE TAKEOVER" (Government Seizure)
**The Move:** The Army asserts "Entire Right, Title, and Interest" (Government Ownership) under 37 CFR 501.6(a)(1), claiming the invention was made during duty hours or with government contribution.

**Triggers:**
*   A "Yes" found in the Commander's review (DA 2871-R).
*   JALS-IP argues that "Cybersecurity" is inherent to "25S" duties (Broad Interpretation).

**THE COUNTER-MOVE (Defense Depth 1):**
1.  **The "Clean Room" Audit:** Present the `ot8o4qzor` notarized audit trail. Challenge the specific factual claim (e.g., "Show me the government computer I used.").
2.  **The "Subject Matter Checkmate":** Argue **Scope of Duties**. A 25S operates Satellites; they do not write Post-Quantum Cryptography code. The invention is *Ultra Vires* (beyond scope/authority) of the assigned role.
3.  **The "Nuclear Option" (Public Fork):** If seizure is imminent, we burn the "Private" bridge. We release the source code to the public domain (Apache 2.0) immediately *before* the title transfer is finalized.
    *   *Result:* The Government owns "Open Source Code." They cannot commercialize it exclusively. Value dropped to zero.

## SCENARIO B: THE "STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP" (License Acceptance)
**The Move:** TjAG acts under **37 CFR 501.6(a)(2)**. They acknowledge you own the title, BUT they demand the standard "Government Purpose License" because you are a Soldier.

**Triggers:**
*   Standard Army policy. They rarely let a Soldier keep 100% rights without keeping a license for themselves.
*   They accept your "National Security License" offer from the email.

**THE COUNTER-MOVE (Optimization):**
1.  **Accept & Amplify:** This is the **Winning Scenario**.
    *   We *gave* them the license voluntarily. It costs us nothing (Royalty-Free).
2.  **The "Prosecution Pivot":** We immediately request the Army **Patent Attorneys prosecute the patent** on our behalf (allowed since they have a license).
    *   *Benefit:* Free legal work (~$20k value) and a stronger patent.
3.  **Commercial Impact:** We market Khepra to the private sector / allies as "The Protocol the US Army Uses." (Dual-Use Technology).

## SCENARIO C: THE "TOTAL VICTORY" (Interest Rejection)
**The Move:** The Army determines the invention is "Made by an employee... not in consequence of official duties" (37 CFR 501.6(a)(3)) and the Government has **NO INTEREST**.

**Triggers:**
*   The "Clean Room" defense is overwhelming.
*   The invention is deemed "Too Niche" or "Too Commercial" for Army acquisition.

**THE COUNTER-MOVE (Commercial Blitz):**
1.  **Full Sovereignty:** We own 100% rights. No Government License exists.
2.  **Sales Strategy:** We can sell to *anyone*, including the Government, but now they have to **PAY** for a license like any other customer.
3.  **Risk:** We lose the "Free Patent Prosecution" benefit. We must fund the patent ourselves.
