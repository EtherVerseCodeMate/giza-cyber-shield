# ANNEX A: PROCEDURAL TIMELINE UNDER AR 27-60
**SUBJECT:** Roadmap for Invention Rights Determination and Retention
**APPLICABLE REGULATION:** Army Regulation 27-60 (Intellectual Property)

---

## **EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**
This annex establishes the formal procedural timeline adopted by the Inventor to ensure full compliance with AR 27-60. It delineates the specific administrative actions required to adjudicate rights for the invention conceived prior to and reduced to practice independently of official military duties. This roadmap serves as the Inventor’s affirmation of procedural adherence (The "Clean-Room" Protocol).

---

## **PHASE 1: DISCLOSURE AND EVIDENTIARY FACT-FINDING**
**Status:** [IMMEDIATE / INITIATED]

### **Step 1: The "Clean-Room" Defense (DA Form 2871-R)**
**Action Officer:** The Inventor
**Trigger:** Immediate disclosure upon reduction to practice during service term.

**Operational Intent:**
To establish a verifiable firewall between the Invention and Government resources. The Inventor submits **DA FORM 2871-R (Invention Rights Questionnaire)** to provide a sworn legal record that:
1.  **Conception:** Occurred prior to June 1st, 2025 Title 10 orders Active Duty service (Pre-Existing IP).
2.  **Reduction to Practice:** Occurred using exclusively private resources (Time, Equipment, Funds).
3.  **Scope:** The invention falls outside the assigned military duties and does not rely on Government Technical Direction.

> **Outcome:** Creation of the primary evidentiary dossier.


## **PHASE 2: ADMINISTRATIVE REVIEW & LEGAL DETERMINATION**
**Status:** [PENDING COMMAND SUBMISSION]

### **Step 2: Formal Request for Determination (DA Form 2872-R)**
**Action Officer:** Unit Commander / Authorized Legal Officer
**Trigger:** Receipt of Inventor's DA Form 2871-R and Memorandum for Record.

**Operational Intent:**
The Command validates the "Clean-Room" facts provided in Phase 1 and formally requests a legal ruling. The Command utilizes **DA FORM 2872-R (Request for Determination of Invention Rights)** to forward the Inventor’s dossier to the Intellectual Property Law Division (JALS-IP).
*   **NOTE:** This step is administrative. The Unit Command does not make the final legal ruling on ownership; they act as the conduit to The Judge Advocate General (TJAG).

> **Outcome:** Dossier is formally lodged with JALS-IP for adjudication.


## **PHASE 3: FINAL DISPOSITION & RIGHTS RETENTION**
**Status:** [PROJECTED]

### **Step 3: Execution of Rights (The "Title" Decision)**
**Action Officer:** The Judge Advocate General (TJAG) / IP Law Division
**Trigger:** Review of evidentiary dossier and regulatory compliance.

**Operational Intent:**
Based on the evidence, TJAG issues a legal determination. There are two potential positive outcomes for the Inventor:

#### **SCENARIO A: Full Rights Retention (The "Clean Room" Victory)**
**Goal:** Determination that the **Government has NO Rights.**
**Regulatory Basis:** **37 CFR 501.6(a)(3)**.
*   **Condition:** The invention was made *entirely* on own time, with *no* government resources, and is *not* related to official duties.
*   **Outcome:**
    *   **No DA Form 2873-R is signed.** 
    *   The Army issues a determination stating it has **zero interest** in the invention.
    *   The Inventor retains **100% of Commercial and Governmental Rights**.

#### **SCENARIO B: Title Retention with Government License (Standard Fallback)**
**Goal:** Determination that **The Inventor Owns the Rights**, subject to a Government License.
**Regulatory Basis:** **37 CFR 501.6(a)(2)**.
*   **Condition:** The Army concedes ownership but asserts a connection to service (e.g., broad interpretation of "duties").
*   **Outcome:** Execution of **DA FORM 2873-R (License of Invention)**.
    *   The Inventor grants the Government a "nonexclusive, irrevocable, royalty-free license" strictly for **governmental purposes**.
    *   **CRITICAL RESULT:** The Inventor still retains **100% of Commercial Rights** (Right to Sell, License to Civilians, and Patent privately).
