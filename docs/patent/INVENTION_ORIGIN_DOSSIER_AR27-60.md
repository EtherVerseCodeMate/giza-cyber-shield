### 2. Governing Legal Framework: Rebutting Presumption of Ownership (AR 27–60 Anchor)
The criteria for Government ownership are established in AR 27–60, paragraph 2–12(a). The U.S. Government requires assignment of title if any of the following conditions are present:
*   (1) **Made During Working Hours:** The invention was conceived or reduced to practice during official duty time.
*   (2) **Government Contribution:** The invention was made with a contribution of Government facilities, equipment, materials, funds, or information.
*   (3) **Direct Relation to Duties:** The invention bears a direct relation to or is made in consequence of the official duties of the inventor.

**Legal Standard:** Where none of these conditions are present, the entire right, title, and interest in and to the invention shall be left to the employee.

This document establishes that the KHEPRA Protocol meets the stringent criteria for an **Employee-Owned Invention**.
