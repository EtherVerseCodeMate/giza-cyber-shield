MEMORANDUM FOR RECORD

SUBJECT: Disclosure of Invention Origin and Ownership Determination under AR 27-60 regarding "KHEPRA Protocol"

1.  **PURPOSE:** To formally disclose the conception of the invention titled **"KHEPRA Protocol: Agentic Security Attestation Framework (ASAF) using Adinkra Symbol-Based Post-Quantum Cryptography"** and to initiate a formal rights determination as required by Army Regulation (AR) 27-60, *Intellectual Property*.

2.  **REGULATORY REQUIREMENT:** Per AR 27-60, paragraph 2-1 and 37 CFR Part 501, Department of the Army personnel are required to furnish a full disclosure of any invention made while in service. This memorandum, submitted in conjunction with the attached **DA Form 2871-R** (Invention Rights Questionnaire), fulfills this mandatory reporting requirement and serves as the primary evidentiary basis for rebutting any presumption of Government ownership.

3.  **BACKGROUND AND INVENTION ORIGIN:** The invention in question was conceived, developed, and reduced to practice entirely through private initiative, without Government contribution.

    a.  **Conception Date**: The core architecture was conceived prior to May 1, 2025, during independent academic research associated with the SUNY Albany NSE 526 cohort.
    b.  **Private Resources (Rebuttal to AR 27-60 para 2-12(a)(2))**: No Government time, facilities, equipment, materials, or funds were utilized. All development occurred on personal devices (Air-Gapped VM), utilizing personally funded subscriptions (e.g., AI/ML resources), and strictly during off-duty hours.
    c.  **Lack of Government Contribution**: No government information, technical data, or "subject matter expertise" gained from official duties contributed to the invention. The protocol is a clean-room implementation of public standard Post-Quantum Cryptography (NIST FIPS 203/204) and novel West African Symbolic Logic. The invention reflects the inventor's independent **Academic Independence** research conducted under the **SUNY Albany NSE 526 cohort** as pure academic inquiry, with no Government sponsorship, funding, or dependency on military work products.
        *   **Academic Standing**: Inventor is a Candidate for a Master of Science in Digital Forensics & Cybersecurity, leveraging a pre-service Computer Information Systems Undergraduate certificate earned in **November 2022** (prior to joining the service in April 2023).
        *   **Independent Certification**: The inventor’s subject matter expertise was developed through privately funded coursework (CanCode Community) and State University of New York badges (Python For Data Analytics, AI/ML Foundation, Cybersecurity Risk and Auditing). The inventor independently studied for and earned CompTIA A+, ITF+, and Security+ certifications, establishing a technical baseline distinct from assigned military training.

4.  **DUTY CONNECTION ANALYSIS (Rebuttal to AR 27-60 para 2-12(a)(3)):** The invention does *not* bear a direct relation to, and was not made in consequence of, the inventor's official duties.

    a.  **Assigned Duties**: At the time of invention documentation and reduction to practice, the undersigned's primary duties were administrative in nature (Supply Clerk / Entry Point Controller) due to a documented administrative flag status.
    b.  **MOS Irrelevance**: The undersigned's Military Occupational Specialty (MOS) includes 25S (Satellite Communication Systems Operator-Maintainer). The invention (Agentic Artificial Intelligence Security and Post-Quantum Cryptography) is distinct from the operation and maintenance of military satellite terminals.
    c.  **No R&D Mission**: The undersigned was not assigned to a Research, Development, Test, and Evaluation (RDT&E) activity or tasked with developing cybersecurity software.
