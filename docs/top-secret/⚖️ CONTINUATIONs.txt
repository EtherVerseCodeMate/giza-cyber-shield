⚖️ CONTINUATION #1 — Symbol-Bound Post-Quantum Identity (SB-PQID)
Independent Claim 1

A method for generating a post-quantum cryptographic identity, comprising:

generating a first post-quantum key pair using a lattice-based key encapsulation mechanism;

generating a symbolic descriptor selected from a predefined set of algebraic symbols;

deriving a second post-quantum key pair or key-derivation parameter from the symbolic descriptor using a symbol-parameterized key derivation function; and

binding the symbolic descriptor to the first or second key pair such that any authentication message includes both lattice-based cryptographic material and symbolic descriptor metadata.

Dependent Claims

The method of claim 1, wherein the symbolic descriptor comprises an Adinkra-derived algebraic structure.

The method of claim 1, wherein the binding comprises embedding symbol metadata into a public key comment field or certificate extension.

The method of claim 1, wherein the symbol-parameterized KDF modifies salts, seeds, or noise polynomials.

The method of claim 1, wherein the symbolic descriptor controls permissible trust levels in a zero-trust system.

The method of claim 1, wherein symbolic descriptors encode operational roles or agent intents.

The method of claim 1, wherein the symbolic descriptor determines hybrid ECC+PQC mode selection.

The method of claim 1, further comprising continuous identity refresh based on symbolic state transitions.
