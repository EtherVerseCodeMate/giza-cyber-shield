4. FEDERAL PROTECTION OF TRADE SECRETS
================================================================================

4.1 **ECONOMIC ESPIONAGE ACT (18 U.S.C. 1831-1839)**

The Economic Espionage Act provides criminal penalties for:
- Theft of trade secrets to benefit a foreign government (18 U.S.C. 1831)
- Theft of trade secrets for commercial advantage (18 U.S.C. 1832)

**Penalties**:
- **Individuals**: Fines up to $5,000,000 and imprisonment up to 15 years
- **Organizations**: Fines up to $10,000,000 (or 3x the value of the stolen trade secret)
- **Asset Forfeiture**: Property used in or derived from the violation

4.2 **DEFEND TRADE SECRETS ACT (18 U.S.C. 1836)**

The Defend Trade Secrets Act provides civil remedies for trade secret misappropriation:
- **Injunctive Relief**: Court orders to prevent further disclosure or use
- **Damages**: Actual damages and unjust enrichment, or reasonable royalty
- **Exemplary Damages**: Up to 2x actual damages for willful and malicious misappropriation
- **Attorney's Fees**: For willful and malicious misappropriation or bad faith claims

**Whistleblower Immunity**:
Individuals are immune from liability under DTSA for disclosing trade secrets:
- In confidence to a Federal, state, or local government official or attorney for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law
- In a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit under seal

4.3 **DMCA ANTI-CIRCUMVENTION (17 U.S.C. 1201)**

The Digital Millennium Copyright Act prohibits circumvention of technological protection measures:
- License validation mechanisms
- Telemetry beacon signing and encryption
- Code obfuscation and anti-tampering measures
- Cryptographic key protection

**Penalties**:
- **Civil**: Statutory damages up to $2,500 per violation
- **Criminal**: Fines up to $500,000 and imprisonment up to 5 years (first offense)
